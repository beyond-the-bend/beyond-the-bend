# Listening Beneath the Surface

*By Laura Harvey*

I am still spending time with The Alchemist. Last week I shared how differently this book has been landing for me now compared to when I first read it in my twenties, and I have not been able to put it down since. Each time I return to it, something new surfaces, and this week a different layer has been asking for my attention.

What keeps emerging is how Santiago, the young shepherd at the heart of the story, moves through his journey. He does not travel with certainty. He does not receive clear directives or constant reassurance. Instead, he responds to something quieter: small inner signals that ask for his attention without demanding it. A gentle pull. A subtle sense of direction. Moments that could easily be dismissed as coincidence or wishful thinking if he were not paying close enough attention.

I have been thinking about this because I notice the same pattern in myself, though I have not always known how to describe it. For a long time, I expected guidance to arrive loudly, as though truth should be unmistakable or even dramatic. I waited for the clear and undeniable sign that would tell me exactly what to do. When it did not come that way, I wondered if I was missing something or not listening hard enough. But looking back now, I can see that so much of what has actually guided me has come in far more softly, often through my body before it ever became a thought.

A slight softening in the chest. A sense of ease when I imagine one direction over another. A heaviness or tightening that appears when something is not aligned, even when it looks right on the surface. These sensations are easy to miss because they are ordinary and fleeting, and the body registers them briefly before the mind has time to analyze or explain or, more often, override the experience entirely. When we are not consciously listening, these moments pass through like background noise.

But when we slow down just enough to notice, when we actually choose to feel what the breath is doing, whether there is ease or constriction, openness or something held, something shifts. The quiet signal becomes perceptible. Not as a decision exactly, but as a felt sense of this fits, this can live here without effort. It is simple and honest and tends to repeat itself gently until we are finally willing to listen.

Perhaps this week is not about asking what your purpose is or what the next big step should be. Perhaps it is about becoming a little more willing to notice what your body is already communicating in small moments throughout the day. The gentle softening, the subtle resistance, the quiet yes that moves through sensation before it ever finds words.

With warmth and gratitude,
Laura
