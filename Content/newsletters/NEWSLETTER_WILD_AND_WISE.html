The Wild and The Wise

In simple terms, the energy of the horse is about movement, vitality, and a certain kind of unapologetic strength. It is not about the frantic "doing" we are so often tasked with, but rather about a natural, rhythmic power.

We often think of wildness as something belonging only to youth. We associate it with being reckless. But there is a different kind of wildness that arrives later in life. It is the wildness of a woman who has stopped asking for permission to be herself. It is a "wise wild."

It is the moment you realize that after years of caretaking and sometimes feeling invisible, your energy is finally your own again. You are no longer the horse pulling the carriage for everyone else. You are the horse discovering the open field.

Practice of the Week: Rooting to Rise

Take a moment to sit or stand tall, feeling the soles of your feet connecting to the earth beneath you.

  • Identify where you feel the most weight in your body.
  • Imagine that weight as a steady anchor, providing you the safety to soften your shoulders.
  • On your next inhale, imagine that steady, horse-like energy rising from the earth into your belly.
  • Let your exhale be a quiet release of any "workhouse" burdens you are carrying for others today.

Linger here for three breaths, simply noticing what it feels like to be both grounded and alive.
