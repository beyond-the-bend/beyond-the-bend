# The Wild and The Wise

*By Laura Harvey*

I have been doing some reading lately about the energy that arrives with the turn of the lunar year, and there is something in the symbol of the Horse that I have been carrying with me through my own transitions this week. It is a specific quality of strength, not the urgent, effortful kind, but something more natural and rhythmic. The kind that does not push but simply moves.

We often think of wildness as belonging only to youth. We associate it with being reckless or unformed, as though it is something we are supposed to outgrow on our way to becoming responsible and settled. But I have been sitting with the idea that there is a very different kind of wildness that arrives later in life, one that is not about recklessness at all. It is the wildness of a woman who has finally stopped asking for permission to be herself. I have started calling it a wise wild, and I think many of us are just beginning to understand what that actually means.

For so much of our lives, we have been in service to everyone else's needs. We have been the ones who held things together, who showed up reliably, who kept the whole thing running. And that is not nothing. There is love in it, real and genuine love. But after years of giving so much of our energy outward, something begins to stir. The energy starts to feel like our own again. We start wondering what we might do with it if we weren't so busy handing it over to everyone else.

I find myself returning to this image of a horse. Not the horse pulling the carriage, doing the work set out for it by someone else, working hard and reliably and endlessly. But the horse that has been let out into an open field, moving simply because it wants to, because the body is built for it, because there is something like joy in having the room to go wherever the body leads. I think that is what this season of life is actually offering us, if we are willing to receive it.

That is the kind of wildness I am interested in. Not chaos or abandon, but the experience of coming back to yourself so fully that you move through the world in a way that feels genuinely like you. After years of caretaking and sometimes feeling invisible, there is something radical and deeply right about letting that energy return.

With warmth and gratitude,
Laura
