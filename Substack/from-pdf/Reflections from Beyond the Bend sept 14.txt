I came across a quote this week that feels especially meaningful for this
time of year. As the seasons shift, I think it offers a gentle reminder for
both life and practice:

“Try to accept the changing seasons of your heart, even as you have
always accepted the passing seasons that move over your fields.” -
Rumi

The seasons of the earth unfold in their own time. Fields grow, ripen,
are harvested, and then rest, waiting quietly for what comes next. We
accept these cycles as part of life - natural, inevitable, beyond our
control.

In the same way, our inner lives move through seasons: times of energy
and abundance, times of quiet and rest. Some seasons feel welcome,
others more difficult, but each one carries its own wisdom.

Yoga offers us a way to practice this acceptance. Through breath and
movement, we honor the season we’re in, without rushing ahead or
clinging to what has passed. In stillness, we remember that - like the
resting field - we are allowed to pause, to replenish, and to simply be.

I hope this reminder feels as grounding for you as it did for me when I
read it. May you find a moment this week to soften, breathe, and allow
yourself to simply rest in the season you’re in.

Honouring Your Body's
Natural Rhythm

By Laura Harvey | Beyond the Bend Yoga

I’ve been learning something lately that I find fascinating, and I think you
will too. It has to do with the way our energy rises and falls - not just
across the day, but in little cycles within it. And the more I sit with this,
the more it feels like such a beautiful reminder of how we’re designed to
flow, not push.

The Daily Rhythm

You’ve probably heard of circadian rhythms - our 24-hour internal
clock. It’s the rhythm that helps us wake up with the light, feel sleepy as
darkness comes, and keeps our hormones, temperature, and even
digestion in balance. When we honor it - by getting morning light, having
a steady sleep routine, and winding down at night - life feels smoother.
When we fight it (hello, late-night screens or too much coffee), we
usually feel it the next day.

🌙 Chronotypes — Night Owls & Early Birds
One thing I’ve learned is that not all of us are wired the same when it
comes to sleep and energy Some people are natural early birds wide
comes to sleep and energy. Some people are natural early birds - wide
awake with the sunrise. Others, like me, are more night owls - we come
alive later in the evening, and mornings feel slower.

Science even has a word for this: chronotypes. It’s basically your
body’s natural preference for when you feel most alert. And it’s not
something you’re doing “wrong” - it’s part genetics, part life rhythm, and
often just how you’re built.

For me, I’ve accepted that I work best if I go to sleep around midnight
and wake up closer to eight. If I get my 7–8 hours, I feel good. What
matters most isn’t matching someone else’s clock - it’s finding
consistency and honoring the rhythm that actually feels right in your
body.

The Smaller Cycles

But here’s the part that really caught my attention: within that big daily
rhythm are smaller cycles, called ultradian rhythms. These last about
90 minutes. For about an hour and a half, we can be focused, alert, and
energized. Then, almost like clockwork, our bodies ask for a pause.
That’s when you notice yourself yawning, staring out the window, or
craving a snack.

Most of us push through that dip, but our bodies are actually asking us
to stop for just a few minutes, so we can reset for the next wave of
energy. If we listen, we move into the next cycle refreshed. If we ignore
it, stress builds and everything feels harder.

Listening Differently

To me, this feels like such a kind design. It’s as if our bodies are
whispering: you don’t have to go nonstop. You’re built to have seasons
within your day - little summers of focus, little winters of rest.

And this is where our practice comes in. A short meditation. A few deep
breaths. Even closing your eyes for two minutes and doing nothing.
These tiny pauses are not indulgences, they’re how our bodies naturally
come back into balance.

✨ Practice: The Two-Minute Pause
The next time you feel your energy dip, instead of pushing through:

   1. Stop what you’re doing. Place your feet on the ground or settle
     into your chair.

   2. Close your eyes. Rest one hand on your belly, the other on your
     heart.
   3. Inhale gently through your nose.

   4. Pause at the top. Hold for a soft count of three.

   5. Exhale slowly with a sigh - “Haaaaa.” Let it spill out audibly, like
     a long release. Try to make the exhale much longer than the
     inhale.

   6. Repeat for 4–5 breath cycles.

Notice the shift. Shoulders soften, jaw loosens, and your body naturally
resets.

Just like the outer seasons teach us to honor change, our inner rhythms
teach us to honor rest. The next time you notice yourself fading, try
seeing it not as a flaw, but as an invitation. You are allowed to pause,
replenish, and then begin again.

                  ✨ Practice of the Week:
                      Morning Light Ritual
This week, I invite you to start your day with a simple but powerful
practice: getting natural morning light. It may sound small, but it can
completely reset your body’s inner clock. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Step Outside

     Within an hour of waking, go outside (even if it’s cloudy).

     Leave your sunglasses off (unless medically necessary). Natural
     light, even on overcast days, is much stronger than indoor light.

Step 2: Open Your Eyes to the Day

     Spend 5–10 minutes simply being in the light. If the sun is low,
     let your eyes (not directly on the sun) take in the brightness.

     This signals your brain: “It’s daytime now” and starts your
     circadian rhythm for the day.

Step 3: Add Movement or Stillness

Choose what feels right for you:

     Movement: Walk slowly, stretch your arms overhead, roll your
     shoulders, or sway gently.

     Stillness: Stand barefoot on the earth or sit quietly, breathing
     deeply and taking in the morning.

Step 4: Pair with Breath

Try this simple breathing rhythm while you’re outside:

     Inhale through the nose for a count of 4.

     Pause gently at the top.

     Exhale through the nose or mouth for a count of 6.

     Repeat 5 times.

Step 5: Set an Intention

As you soak in the morning light, whisper a simple phrase to yourself -

something like:

     “Today, I welcome clarity.”

     “Today, I honour my energy.”

     “Today, I begin with ease.”

🌞 Why This Matters
     Boosts energy: Morning light tells your body to release cortisol
     (the healthy morning kind) and sets your metabolism in motion.

     Improves mood: Natural light increases serotonin, which helps
     stabilize emotions.

     Better sleep at night: This morning ritual sets a timer in your
     brain so melatonin (your sleep hormone) will rise about 14–16
     hours later - helping you fall asleep more easily.

     Regulates your body clock: Over time, this ritual strengthens
     your circadian rhythm, making your energy more steady
     throughout the day.

🌿 Even 5 minutes can make a difference, but 10–15 is ideal. Try it for
one week and notice how your body feels - more awake in the morning,
steadier during the day, and more ready for rest at night.
