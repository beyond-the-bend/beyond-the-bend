What You Need Before Starting

Step 0: Design Your Templates (One-Time Setup)

Dianne's Task: Before processing your first video, go into Canva and design the **Intro** and **Outro** templates. Follow the "Brand & Operations Guide" for fonts (Playfair Display/Lato) and colours. Choose soft, grounded imagery that matches the brand vibe.
  • CapCut Desktop (free) — download from capcut.com
  • Access to the shared Google Drive folder where Laura drops the edited videos
  • Your Canva Templates: Have your designed Intro/Outro cards ready to export from Canva.
  • Musical Assets: Choose 1-2 calm, ambient music tracks from CapCut's library (or external copyright-free sources) to use for all intros.
  • Access to GoHighLevel (GHL)
Save the intro image, outro image, and music file in a folder on your computer. You will reuse them for every single video, so keep them somewhere easy to find.

Step 1: Download the Video from Google Drive

  1. Open the shared Google Drive folder (Laura will share the link with you)
  2. Look for any video files you haven't processed yet
  3. Right-click the file → Download
  4. Save it to a folder on your computer (suggestion: create a folder called "BTB Videos")

If there are no new videos in the folder, message Laura. Do not go looking elsewhere.

Step 2: Open the Video in CapCut

  1. Open CapCut Desktop
  2. Click New Project
  3. Click Import (or drag the video file into the media area)
  4. Drag the video down onto the timeline at the bottom

You should now see the full class video on your timeline. Don't edit the class itself. Just add the intro and outro.

Step 3: Add the Branded Intro

The intro is a still image placed at the very beginning of the video.

What the intro includes:

  • Beyond the Bend logo
  • Laura's name
  • Welcome text (asking students to gather props, settle in)

How to add it in CapCut:

  1. Design/Export your intro card from Canva as a high-quality PNG.
  2. Import that image file into your media in CapCut.
  3. Drag it onto the timeline before the class video (to the left of it)
  4. Click on the intro image in the timeline
  5. Drag the right edge to set the duration to approximately 15 to 20 seconds
  6. Choose/Import a calm music track from CapCut’s audio library.
  7. Drag the music onto the audio track below the intro image
  8. Trim the music so it only plays during the intro (not during the class)
  9. Fade the music out at the end (select the audio clip → look for fade-out option)

Rules:

  • Music plays ONLY during the intro, never during the class itself
  • The transition from intro to class should feel smooth and calm
  • Make sure there is no gap between the intro and the class video

Step 4: Add the Branded Outro

The outro is a still image placed at the very end.

What the outro includes:

  • A gentle closing message (thank you)
  • Beyond the Bend logo
  • Optional: website URL or social handle

How to add it in CapCut:

  1. Import the outro image file into your media (if not already imported)
  2. Drag it onto the timeline after the class video (to the right of it)
  3. Set the duration to approximately 8 to 10 seconds
  4. Make sure there is no gap between the class video and the outro

Step 5: Quality Check

Before exporting, watch these key moments:

  • ☐ Press Play from the very beginning — does the intro image show correctly?
  • ☐ Does the intro music play and fade out before the class starts?
  • ☐ Is there a clean transition from intro to class (no gap, no overlap)?
  • ☐ Skip to the end — does the outro appear after the class?
  • ☐ Is there a clean transition from class to outro?

You do NOT need to watch the entire 60-minute class. Just check the beginning and end.

Step 6: Export from CapCut

  1. Click the Export button (top right corner)
  2. Set Resolution to 1080p
  3. Set Format to MP4
  4. Set Quality to High
  5. Click Export
  6. Name the file: YYYY-MM-DD_ClassName
    Example: 2025-01-15_GroundedFlow

Export will take several minutes for a 60-minute video. You can work on other tasks while it processes.

Step 7: Upload for Approval (In Google Drive)

  1. Upload your finished, exported MP4 to the "FOR APPROVAL" folder in your shared Google Drive.
  2. Send a quick message to Laura: "The Grounded Flow video for Feb 10 is in the For Approval folder and ready for you to check!"
  3. Wait for Laura's "Green Light" before moving to the next step.

Step 8: Upload to GoHighLevel (GHL)

Note: Only do this once Laura has approved the video in Step 7.

  1. Log into GoHighLevel
  2. Go to Memberships (look in the left sidebar, under "Sites")
  3. Find The Sanctuary membership (or whatever membership Laura has set up)
  4. Open the category where this video belongs
    • If you're not sure which category, message Laura
    • If there's no category yet, message Laura before creating one
  5. Click Add Lesson or Add New Lesson
  6. Lesson Title: Use a clear, warm name. Examples:
    • "Grounded Flow: February 10"
    • "Restorative Stillness: January 22"
    • "Gentle Movement for Anxious Days"
  7. Upload the MP4 from Step 6
  8. Add a short description (1 to 2 sentences). Example:
    "A gentle, grounded flow class to help you come back to yourself. Props: a block and a bolster if you have one."
  9. Save the lesson
  10. Preview it to make sure the video plays
First time uploading to GHL? Explore the membership area and familiarize yourself with where things are. If anything is confusing or doesn't look right, message Laura before making changes. It's always better to ask than guess.

Step 9: Update the Shared Video Tracker

We use a shared Google Sheet for tracking to ensure both Laura and Dianne can see real-time progress. Dianne, please create this sheet and share it with Laura.

Recommended Google Sheet Columns:

  • Class Name
  • Date Recorded
  • Laura Exported? (Yes/No)
  • Dianne Intro/Outro? (Yes/No)
  • Current Status: (e.g., Drafting / For Approval / Approved / Uploaded to GHL)
  • GHL Lesson Link: (for quick reference)
Always update the status immediately after moving an MP4 to "For Approval" or finishing a GHL upload. This keeps Laura's inbox clear of "where are we at?" questions!

Laura fills in the Class Name and Date columns. Dianne checks off each step as she completes it.
