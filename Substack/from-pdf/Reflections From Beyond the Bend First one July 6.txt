This Week’s Wellness Wisdom

Your Nervous System Isn’t a Problem — It’s a Messenger

You don’t need to fix your nervous system, you need to understand what it’s
asking for.

So often, we feel overwhelmed, burnt out, anxious, or stuck… and we blame
ourselves. We tell ourselves to “just calm down” or “get it together.” But what if
those symptoms aren’t signs of failure, but signs of wisdom?

Your nervous system is like a messenger. It’s not broken, it’s communicating.

When you’re constantly in fight, flight, freeze, or fawn, it’s usually because
something inside you doesn’t feel safe. And “safety” isn’t just physical. It’s
emotional, relational, spiritual. It’s about whether you feel seen, heard, held, and
resourced.

In midlife — especially with hormonal shifts, changing identities, and caregiving
roles — the system gets louder. But louder doesn’t mean broken.

🌀 Try This: A 60-Second Reset
Here’s one of my favorite micro-practices you can try right now.

    1. Place your hand gently over your heart or your belly.

    2. Take a slow, intentional inhale through your nose (count of 4).

    3. Exhale gently out the mouth (count of 6).

    4. Repeat 3 times, and silently say:
       “I hear you. I’m listening.”

The more we listen instead of override, the more our nervous systems begin to
