Listening Beneath the Surface

By Laura Harvey | Beyond the Bend Yoga

For a long time, I expected guidance to arrive loudly, as if truth should
be unmistakable or even dramatic. I waited for that clear, undeniable
sign that would tell me exactly what to do. And when it didn't come that
way, I wondered if I was missing something or not listening hard
enough.

But lately, I'm seeing something different. When I'm honest about how I
actually navigate my own choices, so much of what guides me comes in
far more softly, often through my body before it ever becomes a thought.

A slight softening in my chest. A sense of ease when I imagine one

direction over another. A heaviness or tightening that appears when
something isn't aligned, even if it looks right on the surface.

These sensations are easy to miss because they are ordinary and
fleeting. The body registers them quickly, before the mind has time to
analyze, explain, or override the experience. When we aren't
consciously listening, these moments pass through like background
noise.

But when we slow down just enough to notice breath, tension, warmth,
or contraction, something shifts. The quiet yes becomes perceptible, not
as a decision, but as a felt sense.

This kind of knowing doesn't arrive with urgency or drama. It doesn't
inflate the ego or make us feel special or chosen. More often, it feels
like settling, like something inside saying this fits, this can live here
without effort. It is simple. It is honest. And it tends to repeat itself gently
until we are willing to listen.

Perhaps this week isn't about asking what your purpose is or what the
next big step should be. Perhaps it's about becoming a little more
conscious of what your body is already communicating in small
moments throughout the day. Notice the gentle softening. Notice the
subtle resistance. Notice the quiet yes that moves through sensation
before it ever finds words.

OUR WEEKLY PRACTICE:

Let the Body Answer
This isn't something to do all at once. It's something I'm inviting you to
weave gently through your days.

Each day this week, choose one small moment where a choice is
present. Nothing big or life-altering. Something ordinary. What to
respond to first. Whether to say yes or no to an invitation. When to
pause. When to move on.

When you notice the choice, don't decide right away.

Instead, pause for just a few seconds and bring your attention into your
body. Take one slow breath and notice what happens, without trying to
interpret it.

As you imagine one option, notice what your body does. Does anything
soften? Does your breath deepen? Does something settle or open?

Then imagine the other option. Notice again. Is there tightening?
Heaviness? A subtle pulling away? A quiet contraction?

Don't label these sensations as right or wrong. Don't try to be certain.
Just notice.

If it feels available, gently place a hand on your chest or belly and
silently ask yourself: What feels easier to live inside of?

Then stop asking.

Let the answer arrive, or not. Sometimes the quiet yes shows up
immediately. Sometimes it arrives later as a sense of relief, clarity, or
settling.

This practice isn't about making the perfect choice. It's about learning
to recognize the language your body is already speaking.

Over time, you may notice that the quiet yes repeats itself. It's patient.
It doesn't rush. It simply waits until you're willing to listen.
