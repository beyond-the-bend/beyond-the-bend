If you are like me, September arrives with its own kind of buzz. For many of us, it
stirs up the old rhythm of back-to-school, fresh starts, new schedules — and with
it, a rush of "shoulds" and self-imposed pressure.

Suddenly our minds are full of lists: things to organize, tasks to complete, details
that must get handled. And if we don’t tick every box, the inner critic shows up:
“Why can’t I keep up?”

But here’s the thing: stress doesn’t make the work go faster. Busyness doesn’t
always mean productivity. Often, it’s just noise.

What if September could feel different this year?

What if, instead of being pulled into the storm, you brought a little spaciousness
into your days? A pause between the tasks. A few deep breaths before the next
thing. A moment to remember that peace isn’t earned after the list is done, it’s
available right here, in the middle of it all.

Because when the mind is calmer, the noise fades. And when the noise fades,
what truly matters has room to rise.

My invitation for you this month: Notice where you’re choosing “busy” over
“peace,” and gently ask yourself ... is this stress actually helping me?

Returning to the Calm Within

By Laura Harvey | Beyond the Bend Yoga

As I’ve been moving through September’s buzz, I’ve found myself returning to
something I studied several years ago called the Three Principles. They’re a way
of understanding human experience that I first learned through the work of Michael
Neill, and they’ve been on my mind again lately.

The idea goes like this: we don’t experience the world exactly as it is, we
experience it through our own thoughts, moment by moment. That’s why two
people can go through the same situation and feel it so differently.

For me, this perspective is a reminder that the “noise” I feel in September isn’t
really coming from my calendar or my to-do list, it’s being created inside my own
mind. And just like the weather, those inner storms shift and clear on their own.

The gift is knowing that peace is always close by. I don’t have to wait until the list
is finished or the calendar lightens up. A pause, a breath, a simple shift in
awareness can reconnect me with the calm that’s built in.

So this week, when the buzz feels loud, maybe try pausing for a moment. Notice
your shoulders and let them soften. Feel your breath as it moves in and out. Place
a hand on your heart and simply ask yourself, “Is this noise really coming from out
there, or is it just a storm passing through my own mind?”
In that pause, see if you can glimpse the calm that’s always beneath the storm. It
doesn’t take long, just a few breaths, a shift of awareness, and you may find
yourself moving forward with a little more clarity, a little more ease, even in the
middle of it all.

Because peace isn’t something you earn once the work is done. It’s something
you can return to, again and again, right here in the midst of your day.
