The Garden Within

By Laura Harvey | Beyond the Bend Yoga

There’s a quote I love:

“You can only meet someone as deeply as you’ve met yourself.” - Matt
Kahn

Our connections are mirrors that are tender, revealing, sometimes
uncomfortable. They reflect how we speak to ourselves, how safe we
feel in our own company, and how much compassion we’re willing to
extend inward.

When we begin to see this truth clearly, it changes how we approach
love - not as something to find, earn, or chase, but as something that
begins within. The way we treat ourselves becomes the soil from which
every other relationship grows.

Imagine, for a moment, our inner world as a garden. Every thought,
every act of care, every boundary and breath becomes part of its
landscape. Some days it may look lush and alive with new growth
reaching toward the sun. Other days, it may feel untended and
overgrown with old habits or dry from neglect.

This garden isn’t a place to perfect. It’s a place to notice.
To be curious about what’s growing.
To acknowledge what’s struggling
To acknowledge what s struggling.
To gently tend what needs attention.

When we nurture ourselves with gentleness and honesty, that energy
naturally extends outward. It softens our tone, steadies our presence,
and deepens our capacity to love others without losing ourselves. But
when we neglect our inner garden - through self-criticism, avoidance, or
over-giving - the soil becomes depleted. Even when love comes near, it
can’t quite take root.

Many of us learned long ago to earn love through doing, pleasing, or
achieving. Those patterns were intelligent; they kept us connected in the
ways we knew how. But as we grow older, they begin to feel heavy, as if
the garden we’ve been tending was built to please everyone but
ourselves.

Healing begins the moment we bring awareness to this cycle - when we
pause, take a breath, and ask, What would it feel like to meet ourselves
with curiosity instead of judgment?

That single question is like sunlight breaking through clouds. It doesn’t
demand that we fix or change - only that we notice, and allow. Each act
of awareness becomes a drop of water for the roots of self-trust. Each
kind thought becomes nourishment for the soil of our hearts.

Over time, the garden shifts. We begin to see new shoots of growth -
boundaries formed from clarity rather than fear, choices made from love
rather than guilt, tenderness that doesn’t depend on anyone else’s
approval.

We don’t need to be “fixed” or endlessly giving to be worthy of love. We
only need to meet ourselves where we are - with patience, tenderness,
and care.

Because when we do, our roots grow stronger. And from that fertile
ground, every connection in our lives - with others, with the world, and
with our own hearts - begins to bloom in a way that feels more peaceful,
more authentic, and more true.

And as we tend this inner garden together, perhaps we’ll remember that
love, like sunlight, was never something we had to chase.

🌿 Practice of the Week: The Garden Within
Today’s practice is a guided meditation with built-in journaling
reflection, designed to help you reconnect with yourself through the
image of your inner garden - a quiet, living space within where your
energy, emotions, and intentions take root and grow.

As you move through the practice, you’ll be invited to pause and write
when prompted - gently tending to your inner landscape with curiosity
and care. You’ll explore what’s thriving in your life right now, what’s
resting or being held in stillness, and what might be asking for a little
more light - those tender parts of yourself ready to be seen, nourished,
and reclaimed.

✨ Before you begin
Give yourself about 15 minutes for the full experience. This is your
time to slow down and nurture yourself, to listen inwardly and
reconnect with what truly matters.

Find a comfortable, quiet space where you won’t be disturbed. You
might light a candle, wrap yourself in a cozy blanket, or bring
something natural - a stone, a flower, or a plant - to help you feel
grounded. Keep your journal and pen beside you for when the
meditation invites you to reflect.

🎧 For the best experience, I recommend listening with earphones -
it helps you fully immerse in the soundscape and stay present with your
breath and inner imagery.

When you’re ready, settle in, take a few slow breaths, and click below
to begin.
