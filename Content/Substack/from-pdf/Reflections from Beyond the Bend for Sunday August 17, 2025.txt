7 Gateways to Balance

By Laura Harvey | Beyond the Bend Yoga

In yoga and mindfulness, we often focus on the here and now - the breath in your
body, the stretch in your spine, the quiet between thoughts. But sometimes it’s
helpful to step back and see the bigger picture of how all the parts of you -
physical, emotional, and spiritual - connect.

The chakra system is one such map. In Eastern Body, Western Mind, Anodea
Judith blends this ancient Eastern model with Western psychology, showing how
our life stories shape each “energy center” in the body. It’s not about perfection -
it’s about awareness and balance.

The Seven Chakras: A Gentle Guide
1. Root Chakra (Muladhara) - “I am”

Location: Base of spine
Theme: Safety, stability, survival
When balanced: You feel grounded, secure, supported.
When imbalanced: Anxiety, instability, fear around money or home.
Practice: Grounding yoga poses, time in nature, mindful walking.

2. Sacral Chakra (Svadhisthana) - “I feel”

Location: Lower abdomen
Theme: Pleasure, creativity, emotional flow
When balanced: Joy in simple pleasures, healthy relationships, creativity flows.
When imbalanced: Emotional numbness, guilt, overindulgence.
Practice: Creative play, gentle hip-opening poses, mindful movement.

3. Solar Plexus Chakra (Manipura) – “I do”

Location: Upper abdomen
Theme: Personal power, will, confidence
When balanced: Healthy self-esteem, clear boundaries, purposeful action.
When imbalanced: Self-doubt, control issues, passivity or aggression.
Practice: Core-strengthening poses, affirmations of self-worth, breath of fire.

4. Heart Chakra (Anahata) – “I love”

Location: Center of chest
Theme: Love, compassion, connection
When balanced: Openness to give and receive love, forgiveness, empathy.
When imbalanced: Isolation, bitterness, over-giving.
Practice: Heart-opening poses, gratitude journaling, loving-kindness meditation.

5. Throat Chakra (Vishuddha) – “I speak”

Location: Throat
Theme: Truth, self-expression, communication
When balanced: Honest expression, good listening, clarity in speech.
When imbalanced: Fear of speaking up, over-talking, miscommunication.
Practice: Chanting, singing, journaling, practicing honest but kind conversations.

6. Third Eye Chakra (Ajna) – “I see”

Location: Between the eyebrows
Theme: Intuition, insight, imagination
When balanced: Clear vision for life, trust in intuition, wisdom.
When imbalanced: Confusion, overthinking, disconnected from inner guidance.
Practice: Meditation, visualization, time in quiet reflection.

7. Crown Chakra (Sahasrara) – “I know”

Location: Top of head
Theme: Spiritual connection, higher purpose
When balanced: Sense of unity, peace, meaning.
When imbalanced: Disconnection, cynicism, spiritual bypassing.
Practice: Silent meditation, prayer, moments of awe in nature.

Perhaps this week you might choose one chakra that resonates most with you.
Ask yourself:
What’s one gentle action I can take to bring more balance here?
Then, notice what shifts.

                     Your Practice This Week

Chakra Check-In

Purpose:
To connect with each of your seven energy centers, notice where there’s flow or
tension, and invite gentle balance.

Step 1 – Find Your Seat

Sit comfortably with your feet on the ground. Close your eyes or soften your gaze.
Take three slow, deep breaths.

Step 2 – Gentle Awareness Through the Chakras

Bring your attention to each area of the body, moving from base to crown. At each
chakra, pause to notice sensations, emotions, or imagery that arise.

   1. Root (Base of Spine) – I am grounded.
      Notice your connection to the floor. Imagine a warm, steady red light here.
      Ask: Do I feel supported and safe right now?

   2. Sacral (Lower Abdomen) – I feel.
      Sense into your hips and lower belly. Visualize an orange glow.
      Ask: Am I allowing joy, creativity, and emotional flow?

   3. Solar Plexus (Upper Abdomen) – I do.
      Feel the area around your stomach and ribs. Imagine a golden yellow
      warmth.
      Ask: Where do I feel confident and capable? Where do I hold tension?

   4. Heart (Center of Chest) – I love.
      Notice your breath moving through the chest. Picture a soft green light.
      Ask: What wants to be forgiven or embraced?

   5. Throat (Center of Throat) – I speak.
      Sense the throat and jaw. Imagine a sky-blue light.
      Ask: Am I speaking my truth with kindness?

   6. Third Eye (Between Brows) – I see.
      Gently focus between your eyebrows. Visualize deep indigo.
      Ask: What is my inner wisdom showing me right now?

   7. Crown (Top of Head) – I know.
      Sense the space above your head. Picture a soft violet or white light.
      Ask: Where do I feel connected to something greater?

Step 3 – Close the Practice

Take a slow breath in through the nose, and exhale through the mouth.
Open your eyes and notice how you feel now compared to when you began.
