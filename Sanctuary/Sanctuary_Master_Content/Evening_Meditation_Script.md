# Evening Meditation for Rest and Sleep

Begin by settling into your bed in whatever way feels most comfortable.
Allow your body to soften into the surface beneath you.
Let the weight of your body be supported.

There is nothing you need to hold up right now.
The bed is holding you.

Take a slow breath in through the nose.
And gently let it fall out through the mouth.
Again, breathing in slowly.
And letting it go.

Take a moment to notice that you have arrived at the end of your day.
Whatever thoughts or reflections have been moving through you today can pause here for a while.

When we are doing deep inner work, it is natural for the mind to keep processing.
It wants to understand.
It wants to organize the pieces.
Sometimes it can feel as though everything needs to be figured out before sleep can come.

But tonight, you do not need to solve anything before you rest.
The mind does not need to solve everything before you rest.

The deeper wisdom within you continues its work even while you sleep.
As you sleep, the heart integrates.
The nervous system restores.
And the quiet intelligence within you gently settles the experiences of the day.

So nothing important will be lost by letting go tonight.
Sleep is not stepping away from the work.
Sleep is part of the work.

Tonight you are allowed to trust the process.
Trust that the pieces will settle in their own time.
And for now, your only task is to rest.

Take another slow breath in.
And allow the exhale to be just a little longer than the inhale.
Breathing in.
And slowly letting it go.

With each exhale, imagine the body becoming heavier.
The muscles of your face soften.
Your jaw releases.
Your tongue rests gently in the mouth.
Let the space around your eyes soften.
Allow the forehead to smooth.

Bring your attention now down into your shoulders.
If they have been carrying anything from the day, allow them to drop slightly deeper into the bed.
There is nowhere you need to go.
Nothing you need to accomplish.
You are simply arriving here.

Breathing in.
And breathing out.

Notice the natural rhythm of your breath.
You do not need to control it.
Just let the breath move on its own.
The breath comes in.
And the breath goes out.

With every exhale, the nervous system softens a little more.
Like snow slowly falling in a quiet forest.
Everything becoming softer.
More still.

If thoughts appear, that is completely natural.
The mind often continues moving for a while before sleep.
Nothing has gone wrong.
Simply notice the thought.
And imagine placing it gently on a small leaf.
Let that leaf float down a quiet stream.
The thought drifts away.

Another thought may appear.
Place that one on a leaf as well.
And let it drift downstream.

Nothing needs to be pushed away.
Everything is simply allowed to pass.
Your body already knows how to move toward sleep.
Just as it knows how to breathe.
Just as it knows how to heal.
Tonight you are simply allowing that process to unfold.

Bring your attention now to the gentle movement of the breath.
The rise.
And the fall.
The rise.
And the fall.

With each breath out, allow the body to soften even more deeply into the bed.
Your shoulders soft.
Your arms heavy.
Your legs relaxed.
Your whole body supported.

Breathing in.
And breathing out.

The breath slow.
The body heavy.
The mind beginning to grow quiet.
Nothing to solve.
Nothing to hold.
Just resting.

Let the breath move naturally.
In.
And out.

Thoughts drifting farther away.
Like clouds moving slowly across a wide, quiet sky.
The body resting.
The breath slow.
The mind growing still.

If sleep begins to come now, simply allow it.
You do not need to hear the rest of these words.
Your body already knows how to sleep.
Just drift.

Breathing softly.
Letting go.

Resting.
Drifting.
And sleeping.
