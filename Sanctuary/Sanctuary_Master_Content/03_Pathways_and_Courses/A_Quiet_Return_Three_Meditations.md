# A Quiet Return
## Three Guided Embodied Reflections

---

## Meditation 1: Where am I leaving myself?

### Lead-in (arrival only)

Take a moment to arrive where you are.

Let your body settle into the position it's already in.

Notice the surface beneath you.

The way you're being supported right now.

Allow your breath to be natural.

There's nothing to regulate or adjust.

If it feels comfortable, let your eyes close
or soften your gaze.

You're not preparing for anything.

You're simply allowing yourself to pause.

Let yourself arrive.

### Meditation (embodied inquiry with single early naming)

Now, gently notice where your attention is.

Not what you're thinking about,
but where your awareness is actually placed.

If you've been feeling overwhelmed lately,
often what's happening isn't that there's too much,
it's that attention has been living everywhere else.

Just notice that.

And now, bring your attention a little closer to your body.

Not all the way in.

Just closer than it was.

Feel the weight of your body.

The contact points.

The presence of sensation.

You don't need to relax.

You don't need to calm yourself.

Just notice what it's like
to be here, inside your body.

Notice how attention begins to gather
when it has a place to land.

There's nothing to fix.

Nothing to improve.

Just the experience of staying.

As you stay, clarity may arise.

Nothing is wrong.

Nothing is missing.

You're just noticing where you are
and what it's like not to leave.

Let that understanding be felt,
without turning it into a thought.

### Lead-out (settling, safety, completion)

For the next few breaths,
allow yourself to remain exactly as you are.

No effort.

No analysis.

Just this.

You don't need to do anything with what you noticed.

There's nothing to carry forward consciously.

Let this sit in your body
and continue in its own quiet way.

When you're ready,
allow your awareness to widen gently.

Not rushing.

Not snapping back.

Bringing this sense of inhabiting yourself with you.

Not as something fragile,
but as something steady and available.

Take your time.

## Meditation 2: What happens when I stay?

### Lead-in (arrival only)

Take a moment to settle where you are.

Let your body find the position that feels most natural right now.

Notice the surface beneath you.

The simple fact of being supported.

Allow your breath to move on its own.

There's nothing to guide or adjust.

If it feels comfortable, let your eyes close
or soften your gaze.

Give yourself permission to be here
without needing to do anything else.

### Meditation (embodied inquiry)

Now, bring your attention to sensation.

Not to analyze it.

Just to feel.

You might notice an impulse to check in briefly
and then move on.

That's very familiar.

For this practice, see what it's like to stay a little longer.

Choose one area of your body
where sensation is easy to notice.

Somewhere neutral.

Somewhere available.

Let your attention rest there.

Notice what happens when you don't rush away.

When you don't try to improve or change anything.

Just staying.

Sensation may shift.

Or it may remain steady.

Either way is fine.

What matters is the continuity of your presence.

As you remain here,
you may notice a quiet steadiness beginning to form.

Not because you're doing anything right,
but because you're not leaving.

Let your breath continue naturally.

Let sensation be exactly as it is.

There's nothing else required.

Just notice what happens when you stay.

### Lead-out (settling, safety, completion)

For the next few breaths,
allow yourself to remain exactly as you are.

No effort.

No interpretation.

Just this simple experience.

You don't need to do anything with what you noticed.

There's nothing to apply or carry forward.

Let this settle in your body
and continue quietly on its own.

When you're ready,
let your awareness widen gently.

Returning to your day
with this sense of inhabiting yourself.

Not as something you have to maintain,
but as something you recognize.

Take your time.

## Meditation 3: What do I know when I'm here?

### Lead-in (arrival only)

Take a moment to arrive where you are.

Let your body settle into its own rhythm.

Notice the surface beneath you.

The way you're being supported without effort.

Allow your breath to move naturally.

No guiding. No adjusting.

If it feels comfortable, let your eyes close
or soften your gaze.

Give yourself permission to pause here.

Nothing else needs your attention right now.

### Meditation (embodied knowing without urgency)

Now, notice the quality of your awareness.

Not what you're aware of,
but the feeling of awareness itself.

You may sense a little more space.

A little more quiet.

Let that be.

There's no need to look for clarity.

No need to arrive at an answer.

Simply notice what it's like to be here in your body
without trying to direct anything.

As you stay, you may begin to sense a subtle orientation.

Not a thought. Not a plan.

More like a feeling of rightness.

Or a quiet sense of knowing.

This kind of knowing doesn't come from effort.

It doesn't push or announce itself.

It's already present when the body is allowed to settle.

Let yourself rest here for a few moments.

Inside this simple presence.

You don't need to figure your life out right now.

You don't need to decide anything.

Something in you already knows how to move when the time is right.

Let that be enough.

### Lead-out (settling, safety, completion)

For the next few breaths,
allow yourself to remain exactly as you are.

Nothing to hold onto.

Nothing to make happen.

Just this.

You don't need to do anything with what you noticed.

There's nothing to act on or resolve.

Let this understanding settle in your body
and continue quietly, in its own way.

When you're ready,
let your awareness widen gently.

Returning to your day
with this sense of inhabiting yourself.

Not as something fragile.

But as something steady and deeply familiar.

Take your time.
